Black Rose: The Best I've Had So Far

April 2026 — Boston, MA

Tried to get the chowder on St. Paddy's Day. They weren't serving it. Came back. 10/10 — the best I've had in years, and the flavor is one I simply can't do justice to in the words of our simple English language.

Black Rose is one of my favorite bars in Boston — seven nights a week of live music, which is impressive, and on the weekends it is genuinely good. My friend Owen used to live in the North End, so this is his bar before it was mine, and he was with me the day I finally got the chowder.

The first attempt was St. Paddy's Day. An Irish pub, the most Irish day of the year, and their St. Paddy's Day menu did not offer chowder. I came back.

It came out in three minutes flat. I mentioned to the bartender that they must have a vat they pull it from. He did not comment. Which is the correct response — you don't confirm the vat, you don't deny the vat, you just serve the chowder.

At first sight it looked like cream spun with gold and I was skeptical. That color — not white, not grey, that deep warm yellow — is either the best sign or you're about to be surprised in a bad way. I was not surprised in a bad way. They followed the age old ingredients: pork, onion, clams, cream. The thickness was perfect — five out of five, the most perfect thickness I've had in years. The kind that holds its shape when you drag a spoon through it.

The flavor was one of the ages, one I simply can't do justice to in the words of our simple English language. It made me feel at home, as if my mother was making me dinner and the world wasn't crumbling around us actively as we grasped for a piece of wealth. It tasted like how a warm blanket feels on a stormy day, or how the blazing sun feels on your bones after a torrential downpour. It felt like warmth.

The pork is the most surprising thing. They did not use bacon — which so often results in oily chowder, which ruins both the flavor and the feel of it — but rather just pork, as the original recipe prescribes. This is not a small thing. Bacon in chowder is a shortcut and a compromise and the broth knows it. Black Rose did not take the shortcut.

At some point there was a bartender — I hadn't seen him before, new I think, maybe I'm imagining him — who asked me why I loved clam chowder. I explained as best I could. He told me Black Rose was the best of the best. He's right. I don't know who he was. It doesn't matter.

It is the best chowder I have had in years, and for that reason I will come back. That said, I come back here regularly to get blackout drunk and listen to Irish tunes, so they did not need the chowder to convince me. Music does the same thing to me that chowder does — it doesn't need to be perfect, it just needs to be real, and when it is, you keep coming back. Black Rose has both.

10/10. Excel and consume, my chowder loving friends — for today is a day, and tomorrow is the future which we must grasp as we are able.
