Black Rose: The Best I've Had So Far

April 2026 — Boston, MA

Came in on St. Paddy's Day and they weren't serving it. Came back with my buddy Owen. Best decision I've made in a while. 10/10.

The first attempt was St. Paddy's Day — an Irish pub in Boston on the most Irish day of the year, and they weren't serving chowder. I won't dwell on it. I came back another day with my buddy Owen, and that is the story worth telling.

It came out in five minutes flat. Five minutes. Which tells you something — they have a vat going, some eternal chowder operation running in the back, which is exactly what you want. You don't want a kitchen that's starting from scratch every time someone orders a bowl. You want a pub that has committed to a vat and a tradition and is not wavering from it.

The color was the first thing. Yellow. Not white, not grey — yellow. That's the tell. That's how you know before the first spoonful that the people making this understood the assignment. The thickness was perfect — five out of five — the kind of chowder that holds its shape when you drag a spoon through it but doesn't sit in your bowl like a solid object. Onions, potato, pork, clam. Seasoned well. I think there was cayenne in there, which would track — that's how the original was made, before everyone got timid about it. It tasted like someone actually looked at a historical recipe and said yes, that, let's do that.

10/10 — best I've had, and that rating stands until something better comes along. Which I sincerely hope does not happen, because I would like to have an excuse to keep coming back here.
