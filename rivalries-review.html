Rivalries: Thin, Thin, Thin

April 2026 — Falmouth, ME

The chowder was thin. The pork was undercooked. The potatoes were, inexplicably, perfect. 4/10.

The chowder was thin. Thin. Thin. Not thin as in delicate, not thin as in it had the grace of a consommé — thin as in it had somewhere to be and forgot to thicken up on the way out. Clam water with aspirations.

The pork was undercooked. I don't know how to say this more plainly. The pork. Was undercooked. In a chowder review you expect to have opinions about cream viscosity and clam-to-potato ratios, and yet here we are talking about undercooked pork.

The potatoes, though. The potatoes were perfectly cooked — and I mean that without irony, they were genuinely the platonic ideal of a chowder potato, holding their shape, giving when pressed, doing everything a potato is supposed to do. Those potatoes deserved to be in a better chowder and I hope they know that.

Taste was minimal. More flavor than a void — credit where it's due — but it needed salt. Badly. Something in there had something to say and just couldn't quite get it out.

4/10. The potatoes were good. That's the whole thing. That's where we are.
