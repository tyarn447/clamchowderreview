Fisherman's: A Family Dinner

May 2026 — Seattle, WA

A kitschy boardwalk restaurant, a family dinner, a sourdough bread bowl that takes me completely offline, and some ancient truths that surfaced while I was gone. 9/10.

The restaurant is on a boardwalk. Kitschy — souvenir everything, a sign font that has not been updated since the Clinton administration. We're here for a family dinner, which means my fiancé and I are wedged into a table with the parents, and everyone is talking about family dinner things. How was the drive. What's happening with work. The usual rotation.

I was not thinking about chowder.

Obviously I ordered the chowder.

It arrived in a sourdough bread bowl, browned and full of life, and I stopped participating in the dinner. I don't know what was said between the arrival of that bowl and, like, thirty minutes later. I was somewhere else.

The chowder had a slightly yellow hue — which, if you know, you know. Yellow means someone made this. It means heat and care and actual cream and not a vat that has been sitting there since before I was born. It came out hot. So you know.

The pork is like thick ham, crisped up — no bacon situation to report, which is always a good sign. The potatoes had their skins on, which is novel and somehow exactly right, and they were small and cooked through. The broth was rich and creamy and tasted like something. Like clams. Like cream. Like actual ingredients in actual proportion to one another. I cannot stress enough how much this is not a given.

Two packets of crackers. Two. That means they like you here.

I need to talk about the bread and I'm not going to. I'm not prepared to process what the bread means for the review or for this site. Anyways.

I came back to the table — metaphorically — and the conversation had taken a hard left. The parents, it turned out, had done drugs. At some point. In their lives. There were admissions. Ancient truths were discovered at a seafood restaurant on a boardwalk in Seattle. I will not elaborate. This is between us and the clams.

The chowder was not the most surprising thing at that table. But it was the best. 9/10.
