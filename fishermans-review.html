Fisherman's: They Like You Here

May 2026 — Seattle, WA

A kitschy boardwalk restaurant with zero expectations delivers a sourdough bread bowl of hot, yellow-hued, genuine chowder. And then there's the bread. We are not talking about the bread. 9/10.

The restaurant was on a boardwalk. Very kitschy — the kind of place with souvenir everything and a sign font that has not been updated since the Clinton administration. I had zero expectations for the food. We were there — first dinner all together before a family cruise, the whole production. Chowder was not on my mind.

Obviously I ordered the chowder.

It came in a sourdough bread bowl — beautiful, browned, full of life. And the chowder itself had a slightly yellow color, which, if you know, you know. That color means it's delicious and made with love. It is the color of something that someone made, not something that has been sitting in a perpetual stew vat since the restaurant opened. It came out hot. So you know.

The pork is more like ham, crisped up. Good sign. No bacon situation to report. The potatoes were small and well-cooked — and they had the skin on them, which is novel and new and provides a good texture I like. Two packets of crackers. Two. That means they like you here.

The broth was rich and creamy. The exact opposite of the void of flavor some other places have had. It tasted like something. Like clams and cream and actual ingredients. I cannot stress enough how much this is not a given.

Don't get me started on the bread. The bread almost outshone the chowder and I am not prepared to process what that means for this site. Anyways.

Meanwhile, my family had somehow ended up talking about whether the older folks have done drugs. We discovered some ancient truths. I will not elaborate. The chowder was not the most surprising thing at the table — but it was the best.

I think the waitress was surprised. I can't imagine they see many people devour both the chowder and the entire loaf of bread like a starved homeless man. 9/10.
