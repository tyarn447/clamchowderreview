Why All Chowder

A haddock chowder I never ordered, a chicken parm I did, and the realization that chowder — all chowder — is the point.

Life can be a slog.

No less so than when a family member is sick. I recently traveled to Maine to be with a sick family member and will continue to do so weekly. This is not the blog post where I get into that. But it is the backdrop. Keep it in your mind.

While there I had a choice of two restaurants to eat at for dinner. One had chicken parm — another one of the dishes that keeps me from the abyss — and the other had chowder. However this chowder was not clam chowder, which this site was previously focused on. Haddock chowder.

I eventually settled on the restaurant with chicken parm.

Due entirely to the shortsightedness of my own philosophy.

I love chowder. In most forms — looking at you, Manhattan chowder. Had this been a clam chowder I would have jumped at the opportunity of trying it. But it wasn't, and because I had built this entire operation around clam chowder specifically, I let myself walk past a haddock chowder. A haddock chowder, mollusk friends. In Maine. I deeply regret not going to that restaurant. Although my god the chicken parm was good.

But here is what hit me on the drive back.

While we are all stuck on this ball of dirt, dealing with the endless march of time and the degradation to our bodies, chowder has always brought communities and families together over the stirring pot of life. Whether that be seafood chowder, clam, haddock, cod, or any other chowder — the pot does not discriminate. Chowder is a gathering point. It always has been.

I should have taken my family member to eat that chowder instead so they could experience the joy I have with it. We were at the same table — they were right there — but it would have been different over chowder. It would have provided a beautiful meeting point for our two minds to converse over. A bowl between us. Something warm. Something hearty. Instead we ate chicken parm, which was admittedly excellent, but it was not two minds meeting over chowder.

Due to this event — and a couple chowder books I have bought recently, one of which informed me that the first chowders were water-based, which is insane — I have decided to rebrand this site. Not clam chowder specifically. All chowder. Every chowder deserves to be reviewed, documented, and given its due philosophical weight.

All chowder should be loved. Whether big or small. Cream-based or tomato-based (I guess). Or hell, even water-based, as the original chowder makers apparently intended. Chowder is a beautiful healing dish that allows the body to recover due to its hearty nature. It is restorative in a way that transcends the ingredients.

My family member is doing better. I will be bringing them to eat a hearty chowder when I visit next week. We will sit across from each other and there will be a bowl between us and that will be enough.

Also the chicken parm was 9/10. Some of the best I have had.
