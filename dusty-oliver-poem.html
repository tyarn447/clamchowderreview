Cauldron Chowder Dream

A Seattle wanderer, an old typewriter, and a request for a poem about clam chowder. His name was Dusty Oliver.

Vacation can breathe new life into an existence bent on stifling the very thing that used to be widely available to us but has been choked out of most people's lives, adventure. Not the kind planned to the minute — adventure is letting your feet take you somewhere, finding yourself lost in a city exploring places you didn't know existed.

With that said I need to refocus this back to what is most important, chowder.

On one of my little adventures in the city of Seattle I stumbled upon a man who I can only describe as looking like a wanderer of the world. He had a beautiful old leather hat on and he was sat outside a book store with an old typewriter. He offered me a poem, a poem about anything I would like.

My brain stumbled over itself trying to think of a cool niche topic. After a few minutes of trying and failing I realized cool niche topics aren't very fun or adventurous — this man had probably heard the same "cool" topics time and time again and churned out poems about them endlessly.

Thus I turned towards chowder.

I said write me a poem about clam chowder. He looked baffled at the topic but laughed, said that's new and got to writing. The poem of clam chowder came out thus:

Big chunks of clam don't skimp that shit, boiled tater finely cut onions and celery what spellery is this in a cauldron creamy dream a bites like a Sunbeam in winter and I crave it daily it just took a taste and that's all I get til summers back

Chowder should be an adventure too. The best bowls I've had weren't booked in advance or recommended by an algorithm — they were stumbled upon, walked into, discovered the way you discover anything worth having. There's something about a chowder you weren't looking for that makes it taste better. Maybe that's insane. I don't think it is.

Adventure is an interesting thing. It can find you in a crowded market having a man who crowns himself Dusty Oliver writing a poem about clam chowder. Don't let your vacations be planned to the minute, don't let them be safe — leave room for the feet to wander and the unexpected to find you.

Live and slurp my mollusk friends and adventure on to find the best chowder around.
